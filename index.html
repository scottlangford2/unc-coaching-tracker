Private Aviation Intelligence — What to Watch

  • Gulfstreams, Citations, Learjets, King Airs flying between candidate cities and RDU — these are the typical coaching search aircraft
  • UNC-affiliated tail numbers — Search FAA registry for aircraft owned by UNC, the UNC System, or known Tar Heel boosters
  • FBO activity at RDU GA terminal — Watch for unusual private arrivals at Signature Flight Support or UNC Air Ops
  • Late night / early morning flights — Discreet travel to avoid detection; 10pm-6am flights between candidate cities and RDU are highly suspicious
  • Round trips under 12 hours — Quick "fly in, interview, fly out" pattern is the classic tell
  • Horace Williams Airport (IGX) — UNC's on-campus airfield; any business jet landing here is almost certainly UNC-related
  • NetJets / Wheels Up / Flexjet charters — Schools often use charter companies; watch for business jets on relevant routes even without a UNC tail number
  • ADS-B Exchange is best — Unlike FlightAware/FR24, it doesn't filter out aircraft at owner request. Most comprehensive for private aviation.
